Resume Tips

Expert advice to make your resume stand out and get past ATS systems

Tailor Your Resume

Customize your resume for each job application by matching your skills and experiences to the job description. Use relevant keywords from the posting to help your resume pass through ATS filters.

Key Takeaways:

  • Study the job description carefully
  • Include relevant keywords naturally throughout your resume
  • Prioritize experiences that match the requirements

Quantify Achievements

Use numbers, percentages, and specific metrics to demonstrate your impact. Quantified achievements are more impressive and memorable than vague statements.

Examples:

  • Instead of "Increased sales" → "Increased sales by 32% in 6 months"
  • Instead of "Managed team" → "Led a team of 12 developers across 3 projects"
  • Instead of "Reduced costs" → "Reduced operational costs by ₹2.5 lakhs annually"

Focus on Structure & Format

Use a clean, professional layout with consistent formatting. Make your resume easy to scan in 6-7 seconds (the average time recruiters spend on initial review).

Format Tips:

  • Use bullet points instead of paragraphs
  • Include white space to improve readability
  • Keep to 1-2 pages maximum
  • Use standard fonts like Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman

Beat the ATS

Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) scan resumes before human eyes see them. Make your resume ATS-friendly to increase your chances of getting to the interview stage.

ATS Tips:

  • Use standard section headings (e.g., "Experience," "Education")
  • Avoid tables, headers, footers, and text boxes
  • Use simple bullet points (•) instead of fancy symbols
  • Save your resume as a .docx or .pdf file

Common Resume Mistakes to Avoid

Generic Objectives

Replace generic objectives with a tailored professional summary that highlights your relevant qualifications and career goals.

Typos & Grammatical Errors

Always proofread your resume multiple times and ask someone else to review it as well. Typos signal carelessness to employers.

Irrelevant Information

Exclude outdated or irrelevant experiences. Focus on what matters for the specific job you're applying for.
